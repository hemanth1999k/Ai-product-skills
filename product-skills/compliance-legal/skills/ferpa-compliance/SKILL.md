# FERPA Compliance Framework

## Purpose
Understand FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act) requirements for products handling student education records.

## Who FERPA Applies To
- Educational institutions receiving Federal funds (K-12 and higher ed)
- Vendors/companies acting as "school officials" on behalf of these institutions
- Any company handling student "education records"

**Education Records** = Records directly related to a student maintained by an educational institution or its agent. Includes: grades, transcripts, class schedules, financial records, disciplinary records.

Not covered: Personal notes kept by a teacher, law enforcement records, employment records for non-student employees.

## FERPA Core Rights

### For Students (18+) / Parents (Under 18)
- **Right to Inspect**: Access their education records within 45 days of request
- **Right to Amend**: Request correction of inaccurate records
- **Right to Consent**: Prior written consent before disclosure to non-school parties
- **Right to Complain**: File complaints with the Department of Education

## When Can Schools Disclose Without Consent?

### FERPA Exceptions (No Consent Needed)
- **School Officials with Legitimate Educational Interest**: Teachers, administrators, vendors under a School Official agreement
- **Audit/Evaluation**: State/federal education authorities
- **Financial Aid**: Processing financial aid
- **Transfer Schools**: To other schools student is enrolling in
- **Safety Emergencies**: Serious and imminent threat to health/safety
- **Judicial Orders / Subpoenas**: With notice to student/parent
- **Directory Information**: Only if school has published notice and provided opt-out

**Directory Information** (public by default unless opted out): Name, address, phone, date/place of birth, major, participation in activities, attendance dates, degrees awarded.
Non-directory: Grades, GPA, race, gender, financial information — CANNOT be disclosed without consent.

## For Edtech Vendors: Compliance Requirements

If you're a vendor receiving education records from schools:

### Required Agreements
- Written agreement with the school designating vendor as "School Official"
- Agreement must specify legitimate educational interest
- Must limit use to educational purposes
- Cannot use student data for targeted advertising

### Data Handling Requirements
- [ ] Use student data only for providing the contracted service
- [ ] No secondary use of student data
- [ ] No sale of student data (ever)
- [ ] No targeted advertising to students
- [ ] Notify school of any breach
- [ ] Return or destroy records when no longer needed
- [ ] Allow school audit of data practices

### FERPA × COPPA Intersection
If serving students under 13:
COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) also applies:
- Parental consent required before collecting personal info from under-13s
- Schools can provide COPPA consent on behalf of parents for educational purposes
- Strict limits on data collection and use

## State Student Privacy Laws
Many states have stricter laws than FERPA:
- SOPIPA (California): Student Online Personal Information Protection Act
- NYPA (New York): Education Law 2-d
- Check your state's student privacy laws
