# RFP Creation & Response Framework

## Purpose
Create a structured Request for Proposal that gets the right information from vendors, or respond to an RFP to win business.

---

## Creating an RFP (Buyer Perspective)

### RFP Structure

**Section 1: Company & Project Overview**
- Company background
- Project goals and context
- Timeline and key milestones
- Budget range (include this — it saves everyone time)
- Decision-making process and timeline

**Section 2: Scope of Work**
- What you need the vendor to do
- What you're responsible for
- Deliverables and acceptance criteria
- Integration requirements

**Section 3: Vendor Requirements**
- Experience requirements (years, case studies)
- Team requirements (certifications, dedicated contacts)
- Technical requirements (security, compliance, integrations)
- Financial stability requirements

**Section 4: Questions / Response Template**
Organize questions by category:

*Company & Track Record*
- How long have you been in business?
- Provide 3 case studies of similar engagements
- List your 5 largest clients in our industry

*Technical & Security*
- Describe your security controls and certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
- How do you handle data residency?
- What is your uptime SLA and historical performance?
- Describe your incident response process

*Product / Solution*
- How does your solution address [specific need]?
- What is your implementation/onboarding process?
- What are your integration capabilities?
- What customization is possible vs what is fixed?

*Commercial*
- Provide detailed pricing for [described scope]
- What's included in the base price vs add-ons?
- What are your standard payment terms?
- What does pricing look like at X seats / Y volume?

*Support & Partnership*
- What support tiers are available?
- Who would be our primary point of contact?
- Describe your product roadmap process and how customers influence it

**Section 5: Evaluation Criteria** (Be transparent)
Tell vendors how you'll evaluate them:
- Price: 25%
- Technical fit: 30%
- Security/compliance: 20%
- Team and support: 15%
- References: 10%

| Vendor B | 3 (0.9) | 5 (1.25) | ... | X |

## Responding to an RFP (Vendor Perspective)

### Win / No-Bid Decision
Before investing time, assess:
- Do we meet the minimum requirements?
- Do we know the stakeholder or are we a cold vendor?
- Is the budget range a fit?
- Is this customer a good fit for our ICP?

If >2 red flags → consider no-bid.

### Response Principles
1. **Answer what they asked** (not what you wish they asked)
2. **Evidence > Claims**: Case studies beat adjectives
3. **Specificity wins**: "We reduced their time-to-value by 40%" beats "we deliver fast onboarding"
4. **Mirror their language**: Use their words for their pain points
5. **Executive summary first**: Many decisions are made from the summary alone

### RFP Response Structure
1. Executive summary (your strongest case in 1 page)
2. Understanding of their problem (shows you listened)
3. Proposed solution (map to their specific requirements)
4. Differentiators (why you, not competitor)
5. Case studies (relevant, proven)
6. Pricing (clear, no surprises)
7. Implementation plan
8. References

## Output Format
- Complete RFP document (if creating)
- Vendor scoring matrix
- Or: RFP response structure for the specific context
