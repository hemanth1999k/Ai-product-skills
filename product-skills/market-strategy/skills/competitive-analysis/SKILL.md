# Competitive Analysis

## Purpose
Understand the competitive landscape well enough to make strategic positioning decisions and arm sales teams for any conversation.

## Competitor Types

### Direct Competitors
Same target customer, same problem, same solution category.
"A customer might choose them instead of us."

### Indirect Competitors
Same target customer, same problem, different solution type.
Often the most dangerous because they're not obvious.
Example: "They use Excel instead of us."

### Substitute Solutions
The current workaround or legacy approach.
"Do nothing / manual process / in-house build"

Always include "status quo" as a competitor — often the hardest one to beat.

## Competitive Analysis Framework

### For Each Competitor, Research:

**Company Profile**
- Founding year, funding raised, headcount
- Recent news (funding, product launches, exec changes, acquisitions)
- Primary markets and geographies

**Product Analysis**
- Core use cases and features
- UX and user experience quality
- Performance, reliability, scalability
- Integrations and platform ecosystem
- Recent product launches/roadmap signals (changelogs, job postings, blog posts)

**Positioning & Messaging**
- How do they describe themselves?
- What's their stated value proposition?
- Who do they call out as competitors?
- What segments do they target?

**Pricing & Business Model**
- Pricing model (per seat, usage, flat)
- Price point (relative to you)
- Free tier / freemium strategy
- Enterprise vs self-serve orientation

**Go-to-Market**
- Primary sales channels (PLG, outbound, partner)
- Marketing approach (content, events, paid)
- Key partnerships
- Geographic focus

**Strengths & Weaknesses**
Use: Customer reviews (G2, Capterra, AppStore), Reddit/community discussion, churned competitor customers

## Competitive Positioning Map

Plot competitors on two axes that matter most to your ICP:
- Axis 1: [Key differentiator 1] (e.g., Ease of use ↔ Enterprise power)
- Axis 2: [Key differentiator 2] (e.g., Price ↔ Capability depth)

Find whitespace: Where is there an underserved position?

## Win/Loss Analysis

Track the reason behind competitive wins and losses:
- Won because: [Feature, price, relationship, timing, trust, support]
- Lost because: [Same categories]

After 20+ opportunities: Patterns emerge.
Most common loss reason = highest priority competitive improvement.

## Competitive Intelligence Sources
- Product: Sign up for their free tier, subscribe to their changelog
- Market: G2, Capterra, Trustpilot reviews
- Talent: LinkedIn job postings (reveals roadmap priorities)
- Funding: Crunchbase, PitchBook
- News: Google Alerts, Twitter/LinkedIn
- Customers: Win/loss interviews with churned competitor customers

## Output Format
- Competitive landscape summary
- Competitor profiles (1 per direct competitor)
- Positioning map
- Our differentiation and where we win/lose
- Competitive response recommendations
