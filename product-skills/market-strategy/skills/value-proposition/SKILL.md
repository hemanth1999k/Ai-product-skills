# Value Proposition Design

## Purpose
Articulate precisely what value you create, for whom, and why you're better than alternatives — in a way that resonates with your specific customer.

## Value Proposition Canvas (Strategyzer)

### Customer Profile (Right side — start here)
**Customer Jobs**: What are they trying to get done? (Functional, social, emotional)
**Pains**: Risks, obstacles, negative emotions before/during/after the job
**Gains**: Outcomes and benefits they desire (required, expected, desired, unexpected)

### Value Map (Left side)
**Products/Services**: What do you offer?
**Pain Relievers**: How do you eliminate or reduce customer pains?
**Gain Creators**: How do you produce outcomes customers desire?

**FIT** = When pain relievers and gain creators clearly address the most important pains and gains.

## Positioning Statement Formula (Geoffrey Moore)
"For [target customer] who [need or opportunity], [product name] is a [product category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [primary competitive alternative], our product [key differentiation]."

Example:
"For mid-size SaaS companies who need to reduce churn without hiring more CSMs, Retain is a customer success platform that automatically identifies at-risk accounts and triggers interventions. Unlike Gainsight, Retain is designed for teams without dedicated CS ops and can be deployed in a week without professional services."

## Messaging Hierarchy

**Tagline** (5-7 words): The most distilled essence
"Ship faster without breaking things."

**Value Proposition** (1-2 sentences): What you do and who it's for
"[Product] helps [ICP] to [achieve outcome] by [mechanism]."

**Elevator Pitch** (30 seconds): Problem → solution → why us
"[Customer] struggles with [pain]. We solve this by [approach]. Unlike [alternatives], we [key difference], which means [customer outcome]."

**Full Pitch** (3-5 min): Include proof, differentiation, business model

## Value Proposition Testing

Test before betting your marketing on it:
1. **The "So What?" test**: Read your VP aloud. Does it make someone say "so what?" If yes, dig deeper on the benefit.
2. **The "Who Cares?" test**: Would your ICP respond with "that's exactly what I've been looking for"?
3. **The Specificity test**: Replace your company name with a competitor's. Does it still fit? If yes, you're not differentiated enough.
4. **Customer echo test**: Do your customers use language in reviews/interviews that matches your VP? If not, you're speaking your language, not theirs.

## VP for Different Audiences

Same product, different stakeholders need different messages:
- **End user**: Focus on day-to-day tasks made easier, time saved, frustration removed
- **Buyer/economic buyer**: Focus on ROI, risk reduction, strategic value, competitive advantage
- **IT/security**: Focus on reliability, security, compliance, integration ease, support quality
- **Executive sponsor**: Focus on strategic fit, market positioning, growth enablement

## Output Format
- Customer Profile (jobs, pains, gains)
- Value Map (pain relievers, gain creators)
- Positioning statement
- Messaging hierarchy (tagline → VP → elevator pitch)
- VP by stakeholder audience
