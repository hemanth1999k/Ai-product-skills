# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Purpose
Define a complete, coordinated plan for bringing a product to market — or re-launching into a new segment.

## GTM Strategy Framework

### 1. Target Market Definition
Who exactly are you targeting?
- ICP (Ideal Customer Profile): Company characteristics
- Buyer Persona: The human making the decision
- User Persona: The human using the product (may differ from buyer)
- Beachhead Segment: The tightly defined first-market you'll dominate

Narrow your initial GTM. Broad = diluted. Own one segment first.

### 2. Value Proposition
Why will YOUR solution win for this specific segment?
Complete: "[Product] helps [ICP] to [achieve outcome] unlike [alternatives] by [key differentiator]."

This must be:
- Differentiated from alternatives (including "do nothing")
- Aligned with the ICP's actual language and pain points
- Demonstrable, not just claimed

### 3. GTM Motion Selection

| Motion | Best For | CAC | Scalability |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product-Led Growth (PLG) | Self-serve, low-complexity | Low | Very high |
| Sales-Led (SLG) | Enterprise, complex, high-value | High | Limited by headcount |
| Marketing-Led | Brand-building, mid-market | Medium | Medium-high |
| Partner/Channel-Led | Markets where you lack reach | Variable | High with right partners |
| Community-Led | Developer tools, horizontal SaaS | Very low | High |

Most companies use a combination: PLG for SMB/bottom-up, SLG for enterprise/top-down.

### 4. Pricing Strategy
- Align pricing to value delivered, not cost
- Match pricing to GTM motion (self-serve → simple/transparent; enterprise → custom/negotiated)
- Freemium as a distribution strategy, not a business model
- Anchoring: present high tier first

See `pricing-strategy` skill for detailed framework.

### 5. Channel Strategy

**Owned channels**: Product, website, email, in-app notifications
**Paid channels**: Search, social ads, sponsorships, review sites (G2, Capterra)
**Earned channels**: Press, word of mouth, community
**Partner channels**: Integration partners, resellers, agencies

For early GTM: Focus on 1-2 channels only. Learn what works, then scale.

### 6. Sales Process (if sales-led)
- Discovery → Demo → Proof of Value → Proposal → Close
- Define: Who's involved in the buying committee?
- What are the typical objections at each stage?
- What's the average sales cycle length?
- What signals indicate a qualified opportunity?

### 7. Success Metrics

**Pre-launch**: Waitlist signups, letters of intent, design partner commitments
**Launch**: Trial signups, product-qualified leads (PQL), demo requests
**Post-launch**: Conversion rate, time-to-value, NPS from early users
**Growth**: CAC, LTV, payback period, MRR growth rate

### 8. Launch Readiness Checklist
- [ ] Product ready (minimum launch scope complete)
- [ ] Pricing confirmed
- [ ] Positioning and messaging finalized
- [ ] Website/landing page updated
- [ ] Sales team trained
- [ ] Support team trained / FAQ ready
- [ ] Analytics instrumented
- [ ] Launch announcement assets ready (email, PR, social)
- [ ] Customer success onboarding ready
- [ ] Escalation path defined for Day 1 issues

## Output Format
- Full GTM strategy document
- Channel prioritization recommendation
- Launch readiness checklist
- 90-day post-launch milestones
