# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Purpose
Define the specific company archetype that gets the most value from your product — and is most likely to buy, expand, and retain. Not every customer is a good customer.

## ICP vs Persona

| | ICP | Persona |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | Company / Account | Individual human |
| Describes | Who to TARGET | Who to TALK TO |
| Used for | Account selection, marketing lists | Messaging, product design |
| Attributes | Firmographics, tech stack, behaviors | Demographics, goals, pains |

Build the ICP first. Personas live within the ICP.

## ICP Framework

### Firmographic Criteria
Company characteristics that predict fit:
- **Industry/vertical**: Where they operate
- **Company size**: Employees, revenue range
- **Business model**: B2B, B2C, marketplace, SaaS, etc.
- **Stage**: Seed, Series A-C, enterprise, public
- **Geography**: Country, region
- **Growth rate**: Scaling rapidly vs stable

### Technographic Criteria
What tools/systems they use (indicates compatibility and sales signal):
- Current stack (CRM, data warehouse, communication tools)
- Build vs buy tendency
- Cloud vs on-prem
- Existing integrations relevant to your product

### Behavioral Criteria (Best Predictors)
Observed behaviors that indicate fit:
- They already use a complementary product
- They have a role/team that indicates the need (e.g., a dedicated data team)
- They're actively hiring for roles that signal the problem
- They show buying signals (checking pricing page, using competitor, attending relevant events)

### Situational Criteria (Pain Triggers)
The specific situation that makes them need your product NOW:
- Regulatory change approaching
- Company just raised funding
- New executive hire changing priorities
- Growth hitting limitations of current solution

## ICP Scoring Model

Build a scoring rubric to qualify accounts:

| Attribute | Ideal | Weight | Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| Industry | Healthcare / Fintech | 30% | 0-3 |
| Size | 50-500 employees | 25% | 0-3 |
| Tech stack | Uses Salesforce | 20% | 0-3 |
| Growth | Raised Series B in last 18 months | 15% | 0-3 |
| Buyer role | Head of Product exists | 10% | 0-3 |

Total score → Tier A (>2.5), Tier B (1.5-2.5), Tier C (<1.5)
Focus outbound on Tier A. Tier C can come inbound.

## How to Derive Your ICP

Start with your best current customers:
1. List your top 10 customers by: LTV + NPS + expansion potential
2. Find what they have in common (firmographics, tech stack, trigger events)
3. Find what churned customers had in common (avoid these)
4. The ICP is the intersection of "looks like top customers" + "not like churned customers"

## Anti-ICP (Explicitly Exclude)
The ICP isn't complete without defining who to NOT sell to:
- Customers who are price-sensitive beyond your floor
- Customers whose use case strains your product unfairly
- Customers who require customizations that don't scale
- Company sizes where your product over/under-serves

## Output Format
- ICP definition (firmographic + behavioral + situational)
- ICP scoring rubric
- Anti-ICP definition
- Tier A account criteria for outbound targeting
