# Short-Term vs Long-Term Goal Framework

## Purpose
Build a coherent goal hierarchy that connects immediate actions to long-term vision — so teams can make daily tradeoffs with strategic clarity.

## The Three Horizons (McKinsey)

### Horizon 1: Defend & Extend (0-12 months)
Protect and grow the core business.
- Optimize existing product for existing customers
- Fix retention, improve conversion, reduce churn
- Quick wins: high confidence, relatively safe
- Goal type: "Grow [metric] from X to Y by [date]"

### Horizon 2: Emerging Opportunities (12-36 months)
Build tomorrow's core business.
- New segments, adjacent use cases, new channels
- Medium risk, medium confidence
- Goal type: "Establish [new capability/segment] with [leading indicator]"

### Horizon 3: Transformational Bets (3-5+ years)
Explore future disruptions.
- Moonshots, platform plays, entirely new business models
- High risk, low confidence, high potential
- Goal type: "Explore [option] to understand [fundamental question]"

## Goal Hierarchy

```
VISION (5-10 years)
  → MISSION (1-3 years)
    → ANNUAL GOALS (year-level OKRs)
      → QUARTERLY OBJECTIVES (team OKRs)
        → SPRINT GOALS (2-week deliverables)
          → DAILY TASKS
```

Every level should ladder up. If a sprint task doesn't connect to a quarterly objective, question why you're doing it.

## Short-Term vs Long-Term Tension Framework

When facing the short-term vs long-term tradeoff, evaluate each initiative on:

### Short-Term Score (3-month impact)
- Revenue / conversion impact now
- User retention this quarter
- Competitive urgency (does competitor have this?)
- Customer escalations / contract risk

### Long-Term Score (12-18 month impact)
- Platform/foundation value (enables future initiatives)
- Moat building (network effects, data, switching costs)
- Strategic option value (opens new markets)
- Technical debt: does this reduce or increase it?

### Time Investment Mix (sample ratios)

| Company Stage | Short-term | Long-term |
|---|---|---|
| Early PMF | 20% | 80% |
| Post-PMF, growing | 50% | 50% |
| Market leader | 70% | 30% |
| Responding to disruption | 30% | 70% |

## Now / Next / Later Roadmap Framework

**Now (this quarter)**: Committed, in sprint, high confidence
**Next (next quarter)**: Validated, designed, ready to build
**Later (6+ months)**: Directional, exploratory, may change

This beats gantt charts because it's honest about uncertainty.

## Goal-Setting Anti-Patterns
- "Deliver 10 features" (output goal, not outcome goal)
- Goals without owners
- Too many goals (> 3 OKRs per team per quarter)
- Short-term goals that cannibalize long-term (grow revenue through quality-degrading moves)

## Output Format
- Current goal hierarchy mapped across horizons
- Short/long-term balance assessment
- Recommended Now/Next/Later roadmap framing
- Conflicts identified (short vs long-term tensions)
