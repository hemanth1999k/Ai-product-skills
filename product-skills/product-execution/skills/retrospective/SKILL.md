# Sprint Retrospective Facilitation

## Purpose
Help the team continuously improve their process, communication, and delivery — not just surface complaints.

## Retro Principles
1. **Prime Directive** (Norm Kerth): "Regardless of what we discover, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time."
2. Focus on systems and processes, not individual blame
3. Always end with specific, committed action items (not vague "we should communicate better")
4. Rotate facilitation — avoid PM always running the retro

## Retro Format Options

### Classic: Start / Stop / Continue
- Start: Things we should begin doing
- Stop: Things that aren't working
- Continue: Things going well that we should keep

### 4Ls
- Liked: What went well?
- Learned: What did we learn?
- Lacked: What was missing?
- Longed for: What do we wish we had?

### Mad / Sad / Glad
- Mad: What frustrated us?
- Sad: What disappointed us?
- Glad: What made us happy?
Good for: Surfacing emotions in psychologically safe way

### Sailboat (Metaphor)
- Wind (what's pushing us forward)
- Anchor (what's slowing us down)
- Rocks (risks ahead)
- Sun (what's making us happy)
Good for: Visual teams, quarterly retros

### Timeline Retro
Place events on a timeline. Mark each as positive/negative. Discuss patterns.
Good for: Long projects, complex sprints with many events

## Facilitation Flow (90 min)

### 1. Set the stage (10 min)
- Check-in activity (one word to describe the sprint)
- Remind of prime directive
- Confirm anonymity if using digital tools

### 2. Gather data (20-25 min)
- Silent sticky-note writing (3-5 min)
- Share out: read aloud or present on board

### 3. Insights: cluster and vote (20 min)
- Group similar themes
- Dot vote on most important topics (each person gets 3-5 votes)
- Discuss top 2-3 themes in depth

### 4. Decide what to do (20-25 min)
For each top theme:
- What specifically can we change?
- Who owns it?
- How will we know if it improved?

Action items must be SMART: Specific, Measurable, Assigned, Realistic, Time-bound

### 5. Close (10 min)
- Review action items
- One word check-out
- Did the retro itself feel valuable? (ROTI: Return on Time Invested)

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- **Action item graveyard**: Items from last retro never completed
- **Retro without follow-through**: "We said we'd do X" — did we?
- **Blame spiral**: The retro becomes a complaint session about one person
- **Same 3 people talking**: Use techniques that give everyone voice (silent writing, anonymous submission)

## Output Format
- Retro format selected with rationale
- Facilitation guide for the session
- Action item template with owner + due date
- Previous action item review checklist
