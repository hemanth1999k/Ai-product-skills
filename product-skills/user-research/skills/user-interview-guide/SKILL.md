# User Interview Guide

## Purpose
Create effective interview scripts that uncover genuine user needs, not confirmation of existing beliefs.

## Interviewing Principles

1. **Listen 80%, talk 20%** — Silence is your best tool
2. **Past behavior > future intent** — "Tell me about the last time..." not "Would you..."
3. **No leading questions** — "How frustrating is X?" is leading; "How do you feel about X?" is not
4. **Go concrete** — When they say "always" or "never", ask for a specific example
5. **Follow the energy** — When their tone shifts, dig there

## Interview Guide Structure

### Opening (5 min)
- Thank them for their time
- Explain the purpose (learn from them, not test them)
- Ask permission to record
- "There are no right or wrong answers — I want to hear your honest experience"
- Warm-up question: "Tell me a bit about your role / what you work on day to day"

### Background & Context (10 min)
- How long have you been doing [relevant activity]?
- Walk me through how you currently [relevant workflow]
- What tools do you use for this? How did you pick them?

### Core Problem Area (20-25 min)
- Tell me about the last time you [relevant scenario]
- Walk me through what happened step by step
- What was the hardest part of that?
- What did you do when [pain point came up]?
- How often does this happen?
- What have you tried to solve this?
- What do you wish existed?

### Emotional & Motivational Layer (5-10 min)
- When this goes well, what does that feel like?
- When this goes wrong, what's the impact for you personally?
- What would solving this perfectly change for you?

### Wrap-Up (5 min)
- Is there anything you expected me to ask that I didn't?
- Is there anyone else I should talk to?
- Can I follow up if I have more questions?

## Probing Techniques

| When They Say | You Say |
|---|---|
| "It's fine" | "Tell me more about fine" |
| "Usually" / "Sometimes" | "Give me a specific example of when..." |
| "It would be nice if..." | "Have you ever tried solving this? How?" |
| "Everyone wants..." | "Tell me about YOUR experience with..." |
| [Pause after your question] | [Wait. Count to 7 in your head. Don't fill silence.] |

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Asking "would you use this?" (hypothetical, not behavioral)
- Describing your solution and asking for feedback
- Asking multiple questions at once
- Accepting "I don't know" without probing

## Analysis After Interview
Within 24 hours:
1. Write key quotes verbatim
2. Note observations (body language, hesitations, tone shifts)
3. Identify jobs-to-be-done statements
4. Flag surprises and patterns
