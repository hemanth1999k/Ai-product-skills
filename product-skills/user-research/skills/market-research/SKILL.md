# Market Research Framework

## Purpose
Understand a market deeply enough to make strategic product and business decisions.

## Market Research Components

### 1. Market Sizing (TAM / SAM / SOM)

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**
The total revenue opportunity if you captured 100% of the market
- Top-down: Start from industry reports, apply % share logic
- Bottom-up: (# potential customers) × (average contract value)
TAM = theoretical maximum

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**
Portion of TAM you can realistically reach with your model
- Apply: geography, language, distribution channel, minimum viable customer criteria

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**
What you can realistically capture in 3-5 years
- Apply: competitive dynamics, go-to-market capacity, sales cycle
- Sanity check: SOM should be < 10% of SAM for most early-stage companies

### 2. Market Segmentation
Break the market into segments by:
- Customer type (SMB vs Enterprise vs Consumer)
- Vertical (healthcare, fintech, education)
- Geography (US, EMEA, APAC)
- Use case (acquisition, retention, analytics)
- Buying behavior (self-serve vs sales-led)

For each segment: size, growth rate, competitive intensity, accessibility

### 3. Market Trends Analysis
Analyze:
- Growth rate (CAGR) — is the market growing?
- Technology shifts — what's newly possible?
- Regulatory changes — what's changing?
- Behavior shifts — what are customers doing differently?
- Investment trends — where is money flowing?

### 4. Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors**: Same problem, same solution
**Indirect competitors**: Same problem, different solution (including "do nothing")
**Substitute solutions**: What users use instead

For each competitor:
- Target customer
- Core value proposition
- Pricing model
- Strengths / weaknesses
- Recent moves (funding, product, hiring)

Competitive positioning map: plot on 2 key axes that matter to customers

### 5. Market Entry Analysis
- What's the beachhead? (see beachhead-segment skill)
- What's the moat? (network effects, data, switching costs, brand, IP, scale)
- What's the timing thesis? (why now?)
- What are the regulatory barriers?

## Primary vs Secondary Research
- **Secondary**: Industry reports (Gartner, Forrester, CB Insights), public filings, news
- **Primary**: Customer interviews, surveys, expert interviews (industry analysts, ex-employees)

Primary research beats secondary every time for validating your specific thesis.

## Output Format
- Market summary (size, growth, segmentation)
- Competitive landscape map
- Key trends with product implications
- Market entry recommendation
