# Persona Creation

## Purpose
Build research-backed personas that drive product decisions — not decorative wall posters.

## What Makes a Good Persona

Bad personas: demographic fiction with stock photos
Good personas: behavioral patterns grounded in research, focused on JTBD and decision drivers

## Data Sources for Personas
(Always ask: do you have user research, analytics, or interview data?)
- User interview transcripts
- Support ticket themes
- Behavioral analytics (segment patterns)
- Sales call notes
- Survey data
- CRM data

## Persona Structure

### Header
- **Name**: Memorable, not cutesy (Alex, not "Ambitious Amy")
- **Role/Title**: Specific enough to be useful
- **Company type / Context**: Where they work / live / operate

### Demographics (Minimal — only what drives behavior)
- Age range
- Technical proficiency
- Decision-making authority

### The Jobs to Be Done
The core — why they use / would use the product:
- Functional job: "I need to [accomplish specific outcome]"
- Emotional job: "I want to feel [confident, in control, respected]"
- Social job: "I want to be seen as [expert, efficient, trusted]"

### Goals
Top 3 things they're trying to achieve (in the context of your product domain)

### Pain Points
Top 3 frustrations with current solutions

### Current Solutions (Incumbents)
What are they using today? (Spreadsheets? Competitor? Manual process?)
Why is that insufficient?

### Decision Drivers
What makes them choose / switch / churn?
- Price sensitivity
- Switching costs
- Key features required
- Trust factors

### Typical Workflow
A day-in-the-life snapshot: how does your product fit into their world?

### Quotes
2-3 verbatim quotes from research that bring this persona to life

### "This persona is NOT..."
Explicitly state who this persona excludes (prevents scope creep in product discussions)

## Persona Validation Checklist
- [ ] Grounded in actual research, not assumptions
- [ ] Behaviors are specific enough to drive product decisions
- [ ] At least one behavioral differentiator separates this persona from others
- [ ] Team agrees this is a real pattern, not a wishful archetype
- [ ] Includes what they're using today (incumbent)

## Output Format
- Full persona profiles (1 per archetype)
- Comparison table if multiple personas
- Prioritization: Primary / Secondary / Edge case persona
