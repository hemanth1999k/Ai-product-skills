# Research Synthesis

## Purpose
Transform raw research data into actionable insights that drive product decisions.

## Synthesis Methods

### 1. Affinity Mapping (Best for Qualitative)
Process:
1. Write each observation/quote on a separate card (one idea per card)
2. Group cards that feel related (bottom-up, not into pre-existing categories)
3. Name each cluster with an insight statement (not just a theme label)
4. Look for: surprises, tensions, patterns, outliers
5. Weight by frequency and intensity

**Insight vs. Observation:**
- Observation: "Users said they check the app every morning"
- Insight: "Users use morning app checks as an anxiety management ritual, not for productivity"

### 2. JTBD Synthesis
Extract from research:
- Functional jobs (what they're trying to do)
- Emotional jobs (how they want to feel)
- Social jobs (how they want to be perceived)
- Job triggers (what causes them to seek a solution)
- Desired outcomes (how they measure success)
- Current workarounds (what they do today)

### 3. Opportunity Sizing Matrix
For each insight:
- Importance: How important is solving this to users? (survey data)
- Satisfaction: How satisfied are users with current solutions? (survey data)
- Opportunity Score = Importance + max(Importance − Satisfaction, 0)
  (Anthony Ulwick's Outcome-Driven Innovation formula)

High importance + low satisfaction = biggest opportunity

### 4. Persona Pattern Recognition
Look across all participants for behavioral clusters:
- Who uses the product how?
- Where do motivations diverge?
- What segments have distinct needs?

## Synthesis Output Structure

### Research Findings Report
1. **Executive Summary** (3-5 bullets, decision-ready)
2. **Methodology** (who, how many, when, method)
3. **Key Themes** (3-5 major themes with supporting evidence)
4. **Detailed Findings** (per theme: insight + supporting quotes + data)
5. **Opportunities** (ranked)
6. **Recommended Next Steps** (specific product actions)

### The "So What?" Test
For every finding, ask:
- So what? What does this mean for the product?
- Now what? What should we do about it?

If you can't answer those, the finding isn't actionable enough.

## Quote Selection Criteria
Strong quotes are:
- Specific (not vague)
- Behavioral (what they did, not what they'd do)
- Emotionally resonant
- Representative of a pattern (or a compelling outlier)

## Output Format
- Synthesis report
- Top 5-7 insights (with evidence)
- Prioritized opportunity list
- Recommended product experiments
