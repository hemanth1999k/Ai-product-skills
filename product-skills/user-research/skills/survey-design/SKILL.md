# Survey Design

## Purpose
Design surveys that produce actionable data, not just interesting data.

## Survey Design Principles

1. **One question, one concept** — Never double-barrel ("How satisfied are you with the price and quality?")
2. **Response options must be exhaustive and mutually exclusive**
3. **Order matters** — Put sensitive or difficult questions last
4. **Shorter is better** — >10 min completion = high drop-off
5. **Start with easy, engaging questions** — Build momentum

## Question Types & When to Use

### Likert Scale (Agree/Disagree, 1-5 or 1-7)
Use for: Attitudes, satisfaction, agreement
Best practice: Use 7-point for more variance; always include midpoint; label all points

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)
"On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [product] to a friend or colleague?"
- Promoters (9-10), Passives (7-8), Detractors (0-6)
- NPS = % Promoters − % Detractors
- Always follow with: "What's the primary reason for your score?"
- Benchmark: >50 is excellent, >70 is world-class

### CSAT (Customer Satisfaction)
"How satisfied are you with [specific interaction]?" (1-5 scale)
- Use for: Post-interaction, specific feature feedback
- CSAT = (Satisfied + Very Satisfied) / Total

### CES (Customer Effort Score)
"How easy was it to [accomplish task]?" (Very Difficult to Very Easy)
- Use for: Support, onboarding, checkout flows
- Strong predictor of churn and loyalty

### Multiple Choice
Use for: Demographics, tool usage, segmentation
Always include: "Other (please specify)" when list may be incomplete

### Open-Ended
Use sparingly — 1-2 per survey max
Best positions: After a rating question ("Why did you give that rating?"), at the end

## Standard Survey Modules

### Product-Market Fit Survey (Sean Ellis)
"How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?"
- Very disappointed / Somewhat disappointed / Not disappointed
- PMF threshold: >40% "very disappointed"

### JTBD Survey
- What were you trying to accomplish when you [signed up / used X feature]?
- What were you using before?
- What made you choose us?
- What almost stopped you from choosing us?

### Churn Survey
- Why did you cancel / stop using?
- What would bring you back?
- What could we have done differently?

## Sample Size Guidelines
- For % metrics (NPS, CSAT): n=100 minimum for ±10% margin of error
- For segment comparisons: n=30+ per segment
- For regression analysis: n=50+ per variable

## Output Format
- Complete survey with all questions
- Response scale definition
- Analysis plan
- Recommended delivery channel
