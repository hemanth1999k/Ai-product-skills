# Lean Validation Framework

## Purpose
Apply lean startup methodology to minimize waste, maximize learning, and build only what's been validated by real customer behavior.

## Core Lean Principles

1. **Validated Learning** — Every action produces a testable hypothesis
2. **Build-Measure-Learn** — The fundamental loop; make it as short as possible
3. **Innovation Accounting** — Use leading metrics, not vanity metrics
4. **Pivot or Persevere** — Make deliberate decisions based on data

## The Build-Measure-Learn Loop

```
IDEA → BUILD (minimum) → MEASURE (behavior, not opinions) → LEARN → IDEA (refined)
```

The goal: shrink this loop to days/weeks, not months/years.

## Lean Validation Steps

### Step 1: Define the Leap-of-Faith Assumptions
What are the two core assumptions your business model depends on?
- Value Hypothesis: Do customers want this?
- Growth Hypothesis: Will customers tell others? Will they come back?

### Step 2: Build the MVP (Minimum VIABLE Product)
Not minimum feature set — minimum to TEST the leap-of-faith assumptions.
Types of MVPs:
- Video MVP (Dropbox)
- Concierge MVP (Food on the Table)
- Wizard of Oz MVP
- Landing Page MVP
- Single Feature MVP

### Step 3: Define Actionable Metrics
Avoid vanity metrics (total users, page views). Use:
- Cohort analysis (week 1 retention vs week 4)
- Funnel conversion rates
- Revenue per customer
- Net Promoter Score

### Step 4: Innovation Accounting
Establish a baseline → tune the engine → pivot or persevere
- Baseline: What are metrics TODAY?
- Target: What do we expect after optimization?
- Result: Did we move the needle?

### Step 5: Pivot or Persevere Decision
Evidence to persevere:
- Core metrics improving with optimization
- Customer retention
- Willingness to pay

Evidence to pivot:
- Metrics flat despite changes
- Customers churning after first use
- Wrong customer segment

## Pivot Types
- Zoom-in: One feature becomes the whole product
- Zoom-out: The whole product becomes one feature
- Customer segment: Right product, wrong customer
- Customer need: Right customer, wrong problem
- Platform: App to platform
- Business architecture: High margin/low volume ↔ low margin/high volume
- Value capture: How you monetize
- Channel: How you reach customers

## Output Format
- Current hypothesis stated
- MVP type recommended
- 3 actionable metrics to track
- Decision criteria for pivot vs persevere
