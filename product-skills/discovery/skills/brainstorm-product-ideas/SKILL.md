# Product Idea Brainstorming

## Purpose
Generate high-quality, diverse product ideas using structured creative thinking techniques — not random suggestions.

## Framework: 6-Lens Brainstorm

For each idea session, generate ideas through all 6 lenses:

### Lens 1: Problem-First
- What's the most painful part of this user's day?
- What do users complain about repeatedly?
- What workarounds are users already building?

### Lens 2: Analogy & Inspiration
- How does a completely different industry solve a similar problem?
- What would Amazon/Apple/Stripe do differently?
- What would this look like if it were 10x simpler?

### Lens 3: Jobs-to-Be-Done
- What progress is the user trying to make?
- What are they hiring the current product to do?
- What's the emotional job underneath the functional job?

### Lens 4: Constraint Removal
- If time/money/engineering were unlimited, what would you build?
- If you had to solve this with zero code, what would you do?
- What if you removed the most constrained assumption?

### Lens 5: Adjacent Expansion
- What adjacent problems do users have before/after using this product?
- What data or relationships does the product already have that could unlock new value?
- What would a "pro" version of this feature look like?

### Lens 6: Trend-Driven
- What macro trends (AI, regulation, behavior shifts) create new opportunities?
- What's newly possible in the last 2 years that wasn't before?
- What are competitors ignoring?

## Output Format

Present ideas in this structure:

**Idea Name** — one line description
- Problem it solves
- Who benefits most
- Why now / why this
- Rough size of opportunity (small / medium / large / moonshot)
- Riskiest assumption to validate

## After Brainstorming
Suggest using the `assumption-mapping` skill to identify which ideas are riskiest, and `prioritization-frameworks` to rank them.
